About our data

BillScan uses official CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) fee schedules. The Physician Fee Schedule (PFS) covers most outpatient procedures. The Clinical Lab Fee Schedule (CLFS) covers lab tests. The Average Sales Price (ASP) covers drugs and biologicals. The Outpatient Prospective Payment System (OPPS) covers hospital outpatient services. All rates are updated annually and stored locally — no data leaves your device.

How BillScan Saves You Money

Whether you have insurance or not, your medical bills may contain errors and overcharges. Here’s how we find them.

What We Check

Rate Comparison

We compare every charge on your bill against official CMS Medicare rates — the government’s benchmark for what healthcare should cost.

Billing Errors

We scan for upcoding (billing for a more expensive procedure than performed), unbundled charges (splitting procedures that should be billed together), and duplicate charges.

Site-of-Service Savings

Many services cost 2–4× more at a hospital outpatient department than at a doctor’s office. We show you where you could save by choosing a different setting.

Balance Billing Protection

If you have an EOB, we check whether your provider is charging you more than your insurance says you owe — which may be illegal under the No Surprises Act.

Insurance Appeal Support

We generate evidence-backed appeal documents with CMS rate data and legal references you can use to fight denials or overcharges.

Your Savings by Situation

If You’re Uninsured

  • Use the Medicare rate as your negotiation benchmark
  • Hospitals routinely accept Medicare + 20% from self-pay patients
  • Typical savings: 30–60% off the billed amount
  • Ask for prompt-pay discounts (additional 20–40% off)
  • Check if you qualify for charity care / financial assistance

If You Have Insurance

  • Check your bill for coding errors (saves money for everyone)
  • Compare site-of-service rates — choose lower-cost settings for future care
  • Verify your provider isn’t balance billing you beyond your EOB responsibility
  • Appeal denied claims using our CMS rate evidence
  • Negotiate your deductible/coinsurance portion using our data

Your Rights

🛡️ No Surprises Act (2022)

Protects against surprise bills from out-of-network providers at in-network facilities and for emergency services. You can only be charged in-network cost-sharing amounts in these situations.

🏥 Hospital Price Transparency (2021/2026)

Hospitals must publish machine-readable files with their standard charges — including gross charges, discounted cash prices, and payer-specific negotiated rates. You have the right to see these prices before receiving care.

📝 Good Faith Estimates

Uninsured or self-pay patients can request a written cost estimate before scheduled services. If the final bill exceeds the estimate by $400 or more, you can dispute it through a federal process.

⚖️ Appeal Rights

You can appeal any insurance claim denial — first through an internal review with your insurer, then through an external review by an independent organization. Your insurer must tell you how to appeal in every denial letter.

💬 Dispute Rights

If your bill exceeds a Good Faith Estimate by $400 or more, you can initiate the patient-provider dispute resolution process. A third-party arbiter will review your case and determine a fair payment amount.

Can You Dispute Old Bills?

Unpaid Bills

Yes — you can dispute unpaid bills until the statute of limitations expires (typically 3–10 years depending on state). Use the table below to check your state.

Already-Paid Bills

You can request refunds for billing errors such as wrong CPT codes, duplicate charges, and upcoding. There is no time limit for requesting a billing correction, though recovery may be limited.

Bills in Collections

You can still negotiate — collection agencies typically accept 25–50 cents on the dollar. Request debt validation in writing within 30 days of first contact.

⚠ Warning: Restarting the Clock

Making a payment on old debt can restart the statute of limitations in some states. Get legal advice before making any payment on time-barred debt.

No Surprises Act

Only applies to services after January 1, 2022. You must file a dispute within 120 days of receiving the bill. Applies to emergency services and out-of-network providers at in-network facilities.

⚠️ Important Warnings About Old Debt

  • Making any payment on an old debt can restart the statute of limitations in many states. Do not pay anything on old medical debt without checking your state's rules first.
  • Acknowledging the debt in writing can also restart the clock in some states.
  • The statute of limitations only prevents lawsuits — it does not erase the debt. Collectors can still contact you.
  • Medical debt no longer appears on credit reports as of 2025 (CFPB rule). This applies to all medical collections regardless of amount.
  • If a collector sues you on time-barred debt, you must raise the statute of limitations as an affirmative defense in court — the judge won't do it for you.

Source: BillKarma 2026 State-by-State Guide. This is general information, not legal advice. Laws change — verify with your state attorney general's office or consult an attorney.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 27, 2026

1. Overview

BillScan ("we", "our", "the Service") is a medical bill transparency tool. We are committed to protecting your privacy and operate on a zero-storage policy for medical data.

2. What We Do NOT Store

  • Your medical bills, EOBs, or health records
  • Your insurance details or plan information
  • Audit results or reports (returned to your browser only)
  • Uploaded files (deleted immediately after processing)

3. What We Do Store

  • Account information: Email address and hashed password (BLAKE3 hash — we never store your actual password)
  • Subscription status: Your plan type and Stripe customer ID for billing
  • Anonymous analytics: Page views, feature usage counts, and aggregate audit statistics. No personally identifiable information is tracked.
  • Public data: CMS Medicare rates, hospital price transparency data, and community-submitted anonymous price reports

4. Data Processing

When you upload a medical bill:

  • The file is processed entirely in server memory
  • A BLAKE3 hash is computed for integrity verification
  • The file is compared against CMS Medicare rates
  • Results are returned directly to your browser
  • The uploaded file and all temporary data are immediately deleted

5. Password Security

Passwords are hashed using BLAKE3 with random salts via the @noble/hashes library (audited, zero-dependency). We never store or have access to your plaintext password.

6. Payment Processing

Payments are processed by Stripe. We do not store credit card numbers or payment details. Stripe's privacy policy applies to payment data.

7. Analytics

We use self-hosted, privacy-friendly analytics. No third-party tracking services (no Google Analytics, no Facebook Pixel). We track only aggregate data: page view counts, feature usage counts, and audit statistics. No cookies are used for tracking.

8. Third-Party Services

  • Stripe: Payment processing only
  • Turso/libSQL: Database hosting (stores only account and public data)

We do not sell, share, or transfer your personal information to any other third parties.

9. Data Deletion

You can delete your account and all associated data at any time from your account settings. Medical bill data is never stored, so there is nothing to delete.

10. Contact

For privacy questions or data requests, email privacy@billscan.app.
